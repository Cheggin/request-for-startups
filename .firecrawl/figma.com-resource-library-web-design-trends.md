Remember when adding a simple drop shadow to a button felt futuristic? Or when parallax scrolling made a flat page feel like an adventure? Those one-cutting-edge features—dark mode, gradients, playful motion—have become baseline expectations for modern websites.

With 2026 around the corner, new Web design trends are pushing boundaries again. Whether you’re leading creative teams or starting to experiment with your first website, this guide is here to inspire you with the innovations shaping digital experiences—and shows you how to bring them to life in your designs.

## [**Trend 1: 3D and immersive elements**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-1-3d-and-immersive-elements)

![3D and immersive web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/7e4c102c16aacfe7eb8bf36c96698658289736c8-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Designers are leaning into depth and interaction, moving beyond static images to immersive, 3D-driven experiences. Using technologies like WebGL, sites now feature interactive models, scroll-triggered animations, and AR previews

You might see this as a shoe you can spin 306 degrees or in virtual tours that let you walk through a space. Brands like Nike and [IKEA](https://www.ikea.co.id/en/where-are-we/store/ikea-alam-sutera/virtual-reality) use 3D and AR to help users virtually try on products, visualize a fit, or navigate environments before making a purchase.

With Figma, you can use prototyping tools and plugins that mimic WebGL effects to simulate these interactive elements. Try layering effects, experimenting with scroll-triggered animations, or collaborating with your crew in FigJam to [map out immersive user journeys](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/user-journey-map/).

## [**Trend 2: Experimental navigation**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-2-experimental-navigation)

![experimental navigation web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/5c7f99f6cf5cfbc9f9f79c940ad8a70401368a01-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Not all websites follow the standard “Home/About/Contact” path anymore. Designers are experimenting with layouts that feel more like exploration than navigation. Think radial menus, hidden drawers, interactive maps, or nonlinear journeys.

Tools like [Figma Make](https://www.figma.com/make/) and [Dev Mode](https://www.figma.com/dev-mode/) make it easy for your team to prototype these explorations. Check out sites like [Locomotive](https://locomotive.ca/en), [The Outline](https://theoutline.com/), [Palmer](https://www.palmer-dinnerware.com/) dinnerware, and [Google’s Arts and Culture](https://artsandculture.google.com/) for inspiration.

## [**Trend 3: Vibrant color palettes**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-3-vibrant-color-palettes)

![vibrant color palette web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/85d9b6c02d841b9bf6c4ead45f1b65e01885c012-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Color is turning up the volume in 2026. One of the Web design trends for 2026 is that bright, saturated [color palettes](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/color-combinations/) are making a comeback, fueled by Y2K nostalgia, retro patterns, and “dopamine design” aesthetics. Neon gradients, high-contrast pairings, and playful hues are replacing minimal or muted tones, especially on sites for lifestyle, beauty, and youth-focused brands. Look to examples like [Lush](https://www.lush.com/us/en_us), [Headspace](https://www.headspace.com/), and [Starface](https://starface.world/).

## Explore website templates and color palettes for combinations that pop

Uncover new website design ideas by playing with gradients, backgrounds, and overlays until you find a palette that fits your brand’s vibe or story.

## [**Trend 4: Bold typography**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-4-bold-typography)

![bold typography web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/2dd57f8b7e18532deaa0c15699015068a41d76a5-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

[Typography](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/typography-in-design/) is taking center stage in 2026, moving beyond legibility into storytelling. Brands are using custom fonts, oversized headlines, motion, and layered styles to make bold first impressions.

You’ll see expressive types in everything from landing pages to navigation. Hero sections now often feature kinetic lettering, dynamic [font pairings](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/font-pairings/), and variable fonts that respond to interaction or context. ## [**Trend 5: Dark mode**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-5-dark-mode)

![dark mode web design example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/8178e8d88898c66bcc2385fbfc0a8a9f0b2f9cbf-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Dark mode has become standard for sites and mobile apps. It reduces eye strain, saves battery life on computers with OLED screens, and simply looks great when done right. Many well-known brands like YouTube, X, and Slack offer a toggle for switching between light and dark modes. This web design style reflects a broader push for personalization and accessibility.

In Figma, you can prototype dark and light themes side by side, preview accessibility, and test contrast ratios before launch. This level of personalization is yet another hallmark of modern Web design styles.

## [**Trend 6: Motion design and animation**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-6-motion-design-and-animation)

![motion design animation homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/a340ed6f495cb36c3fb3192898bb306d745214f4-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Motion design adds rhythm and storytelling to Web experiences. From subtle hover effects to full scroll-based narratives (“scrollytelling”), motion helps guide attention and build immersion.

Brands like Nike and [Ralph Lauren](https://www.ralphlauren.com/rl-50-timeline-feat) use micro animations—scroll triggers, button ripples, animated states—to enhance the user journey without slowing performance. Other examples include [Jitter](https://jitter.video/), [Sofi](https://www.sofihealth.com/), and [Silo](https://www.silosilosilo.com/). Play with Figma’s interactive components and smart animate in prototypes. Test motion timing and easing curves to match the tone of your site—smooth for luxury, snappy for playful brands.

## [**Trend 7: Gamified design**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-7-gamified-design)

![gamified web design example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/df8f8913d2bfb63e84acadc115f0f8e331bb49d8-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Gamified design is quickly turning websites into digital playgrounds where learning, shopping, and even filling out forms can feel like a rewarding adventure. Think points, levels, badges, progress bars, leaderboards, challenges, and micro-rewards woven into the user journey to boost engagement and motivation.

Gamification encourages users to participate, explore, and return—because it rewards curiosity and makes complex flows feel fun.

[Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/) is a clear example, using daily streaks and leaderboards to build habits. E-commerce brands like [Sephora](https://www.sephora.com/beauty/challenges) have added rewards, challenges, and interactive quizzes to personalize the experience and celebrate user achievements.

## [**Trend 8: Neumorphism**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-8-neumorphism)

![neumorphism web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/7f155a82f958f5ad77c03d03f8407c7f86a43eca-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Neumorphism blends the realism of [skeuomorphism](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/what-is-skeuomorphism/) with [simplicity in design](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/simplicity-design-principles/). Soft shadows and subtle gradients create raised or inset elements that look almost touchable—great for interfaces that aim to feel tactile and refined.

You’ll see this trend in fintech and productivity apps where clarity meets polish. Brands like Apple, [Flow Ninja](https://www.flow.ninja/), and [Stripe](https://stripe.com/) use it to create interfaces that feel intuitive and elevated.

Features of a skeuomorphic design mimic their real-world counterparts—for example, the trash folder looks like a trash can, and the “save” button looks like a floppy disk. By calling upon existing knowledge of the physical world, skeuomorphism makes interfaces easier to understand and use.

## [**Trend 9: Retrofuturism**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-9-retrofuturism)

![retrofuturism web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/e4b04036acbd36a69fdfd93f6e55f91ec27ecf68-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Retrofuturism fuses nostalgia with optimism, bringing vintage visions of the future into modern Web design. Expect neon accents, chrome textures, pixel art, bold gradients inspired by sci-fi films, arcade games, and early Web aesthetics.

Retrofuturism often pairs well with [futuristic fonts](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/futuristic-fonts/) and works especially well for lifestyle brands, portfolios, or music and entertainment sites that want to stand out with personality.

## [**Trend 10: Maximalism**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-10-maximalism)

![maximalism web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/1477814978778db25441e960e947abbf0ffad376-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Minimalism isn’t going anywhere, but maximalism is taking up more space—in the best way. Rich colors, overlapping visuals, bold fonts, and dense compositions are key ingredients in this high-energy trend.

Brands like Spotify and Liquid Death lean into maximalism to surprise and engage visitors, using layers of texture and bold storytelling to leave an impression.

Figma’s canvas is perfect for experimenting with overlapping shapes, stacked effects, and energetic compositions for designs that pop without overwhelming.

## [**Trend 11: Collage**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-11-collage)

![collage web design example homepage](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/f2e8c4e269c89201576628e8719bbba924694d61-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Collage Web design brings scrapbook-style creativity into digital experiences. Think sticker graphics, torn textures, cutout photos, and expressive, hand-drawn fonts. It’s messy on purpose—and that’s what makes it memorable.

Lifestyle brands and personal portfolios often use collage layouts to reflect individuality and playfulness. [La Palatine](https://www.ensemblelapalatine.com/), for examples, blends real photos and digital elements in surprising ways.

Figma’s drag-and-drop tools and endless layer options let you build, arrange, and remix designs until you find the right look.

## [**Trend 12: Neo-brutalism/“anti-design”**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-12-neo-brutalism-anti-design)

![neo-brutalist web design example homepage](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/ad5bf52b1afdce87f6c3396b08df7d783ba7824c-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Neo-brutalism (or“anti-design”) embraces raw, unpolished visuals that stand out in a sea of sleek, minimalist templates.

This style challenges norms and creates a sense of urgency or irreverence—perfect for brands that want to provoke, surprise, or break away from convention. You’ll see it in edgy fashion brands, experimental agencies, and creative portfolios.

Brands like Balenciaga, Diesel, and Mailchimp have used anti-design principles to stand out and create memorable websites.

With Figma, you can easily test unconventional layouts and bold compositions without breaking your design system. It’s a powerful playground for unexpected visuals

## [**Trend 13: Sustainable Web design**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#trend-13-sustainable-web-design)

![sustainable web design homepage example](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/6506f0418b2a384e5e4ea4dccfeb3c5a3c2116a2-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Sustainability is central to Web design trends in 2026 and shaping how sites are built. Leaner code, optimized images, and low-impact hosting help reduce the carbon footprint of digital products.

Designers are also prioritizing [accessibility and inclusion](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/creating-accessible-and-inclusive-design/): high contrast color palettes, screen reader support, voice navigation, and keyboard-only flows are becoming standard. Intentionally avoiding “dark patterns” (deceptive UX tricks) is also part of ethical, sustainable design.

Designers and developers can use Figma collaborate on efficient site flows, test accessibility, and reduce visual clutter without compromising impact.

## [**AI Web design trends**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#ai-web-design-trends)

AI is shaking up Web design, making sites smarter and more personalized. With tools like [Figma Make](https://www.figma.com/make/), AI in 2026 is about smarter, more intuitive digital products. Here’s how it’s showing up:

### [**AI trend 1: Chatbots**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#ai-trend-1-chatbots)

![chatbot example on a homepage](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/de46977dc071fca5d69e0c0c85d230890821f31d-2880x1820.png?w=2880&h=1820&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Today’s AI chatbots are proactive, conversational, and often agentic—capable of handling multi-step tasks and anticipating user needs. According to Figma’s [2025 AI report](https://www.figma.com/blog/figma-2025-ai-report-perspectives/), 51% of Figma users working on AI products are building agents, compared to just 21% last year.

### [**AI trend 2: Voice-activated interfaces**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#ai-trend-2-voice-activated-interfaces)

Voice navigation is getting more advanced and more common, paving the way for more accessibility, hands-free navigation, and personalized help. It now goes beyond search, letting you control site features, talk to helpful chatbots, or browse menus naturally.

Besides Amazon and Google, brands leveraging voice navigation for shopping and site features include Walmart, Domino’s, Nike, ASOS, H&M, and Sephora.

### [**AI trend 3: Progressive lead nurturing**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/\#ai-trend-3-progressive-lead-nurturing)

Smarter forms now learn about your visitors over time. Instead of overwhelming users, AI tailors form questions, follow-ups, and product recommendations based on each interaction.
