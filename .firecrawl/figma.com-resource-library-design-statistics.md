## [**Design employment statistics**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#design-employment-statistics)

Recent data paints a picture of a design workforce that’s adaptable, resilient, and increasingly happier with how they work. These design statistics are a snapshot of how designers are feeling about their careers, their workspaces, and the future of the profession.

01. 41% of [designers and developers](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-and-development/) say they were more satisfied with their job in 2024 than in 2023. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
02. Remote work is now the norm, with 97% of professionals working away from the office at least part time, and over half doing so fully. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
03. In Europe, 71% of designers and 75% of developers say they’re happy with their current workplace setup. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
04. Across the Asia-Pacific region, 70% of designers report being satisfied with their working arrangements. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
05. In the U.S., 62% of designers and 77% of developers express happiness with how and where they work. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
06. 45% of designers believe the job market has declined in recent years. [(Figma, 2025)](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/)
07. The global value of the [graphic design](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/what-is-graphic-design/) market is projected to pass $56 billion by the end of 2025. ( [NMSC, 2025](https://www.nextmsc.com/report/graphic-design-market))
08. Employment in graphic design is expected to remain stable, with a projected 2% growth rate through 2034. ( [U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2025](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/arts-and-design/graphic-designers.htm#:~:text=Median%20annual%20wages%2C%20May%202024,the%20average%20for%20all%20occupations))
09. The median annual pay for graphic designers in 2024 was $61,300, or $29.47 per hour. ( [U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2025](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/arts-and-design/graphic-designers.htm#:~:text=Median%20annual%20wages%2C%20May%202024,the%20average%20for%20all%20occupations))
10. The industry is pretty evenly split by sex: 50.2% of graphic designers in the U.S. are women, and 49.8% are men. ( [Zippia, 2025](https://www.zippia.com/graphic-designer-jobs/demographics/))
11. The average age of graphic designers in the U.S. is 38 years old. ( [Zippia, 2025](https://www.zippia.com/graphic-designer-jobs/demographics/))
12. 68% of graphic designers in the U.S. hold a bachelor’s degree. ( [Zippia, 2025](https://www.zippia.com/graphic-designer-jobs/demographics/))
13. 92% of business leaders expect employees in non-design roles to possess basic design skills and knowledge. ( [Canva, 2024](https://www.canva.com/visual-communications-report/2024/))

## [**AI in design statistics**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#ai-in-design-statistics)

Artificial intelligence (AI) has quickly integrated into the design workflow of teams everywhere. While questions around trust and quality remain, the data shows that designers are embracing these tools to automate repetitive tasks and accelerate exploration. Here’s a look at how AI is currently reshaping the design process.

01. Looking ahead, 85% of designers and developers say AI will be essential to their future success. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
02. Efficiency is the main driver, with 78% of professionals saying that AI tools significantly speed up their workflows. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
03. However, only 58% say it improves the quality of their work. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
04. In the discovery phase, 38% of designers and 43% of developers use AI for customer research. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
05. 40% of designers and 29% of developers use AI for data analysis. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
06. In the design phase, 33% of designers use AI to generate design assets, 22% use it to create first drafts of interfaces or websites, and 21% use it to explore different layouts or themes. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
07. 40% of designers and developers don’t yet trust AI-generated outputs enough to rely on them fully. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
08. Strategic impact is still in question, as only 27% believe AI will significantly move the needle on company goals in the next year. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/ai-2025/))
09. More than half (56%) say AI makes them feel more hopeful about where design is headed. ( [Figma, 2025](https://www.figma.com/reports/designer-developer-trends/))
10. 86% of global creators report that they now use generative AI in their work. ( [Adobe, 2025](https://news.adobe.com/news/2025/10/adobe-max-2025-creators-survey))
11. 81% of creators say AI helps them produce content formats or styles they could not have created on their own. ( [Adobe, 2025](https://news.adobe.com/news/2025/10/adobe-max-2025-creators-survey))
12. 70% of creators express excitement about the potential of using AI agents that can execute tasks autonomously. ( [Adobe, 2025](https://news.adobe.com/news/2025/10/adobe-max-2025-creators-survey))
13. 85% of marketers and creatives report saving roughly four hours per week thanks to GenAI tools. ( [Canva, 2025](https://www.canva.com/marketing-ai-report/))
14. 77% of marketing and creative leaders agree that GenAI tools actively enhance their team's creative output. ( [Canva, 2025](https://www.canva.com/marketing-ai-report/))

## [**Frequently asked questions**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#frequently-asked-questions)

### [**Which UX metrics most directly affect conversion rates?**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#which-ux-metrics-most-directly-affect-conversion)

It typically comes down to page load speed and task success rate. Keeping your site snappy and the path to purchase intuitive ensures users stay engaged and achieve their goals rather than bounce.

### [**Is website accessibility really a legal risk for business?**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#is-website-accessibility-really-a-legal-risk-for)

Yes, failing to meet accessibility standards can lead to legal issues, but the implications go beyond compliance. Building inclusively ensures you aren't alienating the millions of users who rely on assistive tools, making your product stronger and more welcoming for everyone.

### [**How much does color choice impact sales?**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#how-much-does-color-choice-impact-sales)

Color choice is a decisive factor. According to a [2025 Adobe survey](https://www.adobe.com/express/learn/blog/color-psychology-of-branding), half of customers admit they’ve chosen a brand based primarily on color. A consistent [color palette](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/types-of-color-palettes/) boosts brand recognition and helps build the trust necessary to close a sale.

### [**Do I need a degree to get a job in design?**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/design-statistics/\#do-i-need-a-degree-to-get-a-job-in-design)

No, a degree is not strictly required for most design roles. Employers typically value a strong portfolio that demonstrates your practical skills and creativity far more than formal education.
