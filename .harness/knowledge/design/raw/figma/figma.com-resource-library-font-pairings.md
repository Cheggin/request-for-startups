Bring type to life in every interaction.

Typography shapes how users experience your designs, influencing emotions, guiding navigation, and leaving a lasting impression. Thoughtful font pairing creates visual structure, enhances readability, and strengthens your design's impact. But with so many fonts available, finding the right combination of [fonts for your website](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/best-fonts-for-websites/) can be challenging.

The right font pairing helps you create clear visual hierarchies and memorable designs, whether working on marketing materials, social media posts, or entire brand systems.

Read on to learn:

- 39 font pairings to elevate your designs
- Five pro tips for pairing fonts that look good together

## [**Pairing 1: Abril Fatface + Lato**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/font-pairings/\#pairing-1-abril-fatface-lato)

![Example text showing Abril Farface and Lato as a font pairing.](https://cdn.sanity.io/images/599r6htc/regionalized/24be01e219e1b86fceda3a2209ff80a6cc81d501-1000x750.png?w=1000&h=750&q=75&fit=max&auto=format)

Abril Fatface’s bold, elegant serifs make an impact when paired with [Lato’s](https://www.figma.com/fonts/lato/) clean [sans serif](https://www.figma.com/font-types/sans-serif/) design. Use [Abril Fatface](https://www.figma.com/fonts/abril-fatface/) for attention-grabbing headlines and Lato for clear, readable body text.

**Best for:** luxury brands that want to evoke a sense of sophistication or editorial layouts where a balance between impactful topography and legibility is important

**Colophon:**

- **Abril Fatface:** TypeTogether

## [**Five pro tips for pairing fonts**](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/font-pairings/\#five-pro-tips-for-pairing-fonts)

Transform your approach to typography by keeping these pro tips to font pairing in mind:

- **Create meaningful contrast.** Pair fonts with different weights and x-heights to establish a clear visual hierarchy and guide readers through content.
- **Master spacing fundamentals.** Pay close attention to [kerning](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/what-is-kerning/) (letter spacing) and leading (line height) to maintain readability and visual balance.
- **Leverage font families.** Many fonts come with multiple weights (light, regular, bold) and styles (italic, condensed). Use them to create variation while keeping your design cohesive.
- **Prioritize readability.** Choose fonts with clean, clear letterforms and appropriate x-heights, especially for smaller sizes and digital screens. Even decorative headlines should maintain legibility.
- **Test across contexts.** Preview your font combinations on different screens, resolutions, and backgrounds to ensure consistent clarity and visual impact.
